Tesla, Inc. — Q3 2024 Earnings Report (Quarter ended September 2024)

FINANCIAL HIGHLIGHTS

Total Revenue: $25.18 billion, up 8% year over year.
Gross Margin: 19.8%, up from 17.9% in Q2 2024 — the highest since Q2 2023.
Operating Income: $2.72 billion, operating margin of 10.8%.
Net Income: $2.17 billion GAAP, up 17% year over year.
Earnings Per Share: $0.62 diluted GAAP EPS, up 9% year over year.
Free Cash Flow: $2.74 billion.
Cash and equivalents: $33.6 billion.

VEHICLE DELIVERIES AND PRODUCTION

Deliveries: 462,890 vehicles in Q3 2024, up 6% year over year and a new quarterly record.
Production: 469,796 vehicles.
Model 3/Y: 439,975 deliveries.
Other Models (S/X/Cybertruck): 22,915 deliveries.

Cybertruck production is ramping, with Tesla stating it has become the best-selling electric truck in the US. Full Cybertruck production capacity is expected to reach 250,000 units per year.

ENERGY SEGMENT

Energy Generation and Storage: $2.376 billion revenue, up 52% year over year. This is a record quarter for the Energy segment.
Megapack deployments: 6.9 GWh in Q3 2024, more than all of 2022 combined.
Gross margin for Energy segment reached 30.5%, the highest ever.

The Megafactory in Lathrop, California is producing Megapacks at high volume. A second Megafactory is under construction in Shanghai. Demand for grid-scale battery storage significantly exceeds current production capacity.

SERVICES AND OTHER

Services revenue: $2.79 billion, up 29% year over year. Includes vehicle insurance, FSD (Full Self-Driving) subscriptions, Supercharger revenue from third-party vehicles, and used vehicle sales.

CEO COMMENTARY

Elon Musk, CEO: "We had a record quarter for deliveries and energy storage deployments. I'm very excited about what's to come — we have major product launches ahead with the affordable Model and the Robotaxi. Autonomy is the story of Tesla. Anyone who doesn't believe Tesla will solve autonomy should not be a Tesla investor."

"The Cybercab robotaxi event showed what's possible. We expect to start production in 2026 at a target price under $30,000. This is not a traditional car — it has no steering wheel, no pedals. It's designed from the ground up for autonomy."

FULL SELF-DRIVING (FSD) AND AUTONOMY

FSD version 12 uses an end-to-end neural network trained on video data. Tesla has driven over 1.5 billion miles on FSD in 2024. Unsupervised FSD is being tested in Austin and San Francisco.

The Robotaxi (Cybercab) was unveiled in October 2024. Production target is 2026, priced under $30,000. Tesla also unveiled the Robovan, a 20-passenger autonomous vehicle.

Tesla's Optimus humanoid robot is in production at Gigafactory Texas doing useful work. Tesla expects to produce several thousand Optimus robots in 2025 and tens of thousands in 2026. Long-term, Musk believes Optimus could be Tesla's most valuable product.

AUTOPILOT AND SAFETY

Tesla vehicles using Autopilot have approximately 5-6x fewer accidents than the average US vehicle. NHTSA investigations into Autopilot are ongoing. Tesla cooperates with all regulatory inquiries.

MANUFACTURING AND COST REDUCTION

Cost of goods sold per vehicle: $35,100, down from $36,000 in Q2 2024. Tesla continues to drive down manufacturing costs through process improvements at Gigafactories in Texas, Nevada, Germany, Shanghai, and Fremont.

New affordable model (starting under $30,000) expected to begin production in early 2025 using the new unboxed manufacturing process. This model is expected to significantly expand Tesla's addressable market.

RISKS

Margin Pressure: Price cuts to maintain volume have compressed automotive gross margins significantly from the 29% peak in 2022.

Elon Musk Distraction: Musk's involvement in Twitter/X, SpaceX, and political activities raises concerns about focus on Tesla.

Competition: BYD and other Chinese EV makers are intensifying global competition. In China, Tesla faces intense price competition from local brands.

FSD Regulatory Risk: Unsupervised autonomous driving requires regulatory approval in each jurisdiction. Timeline is uncertain.

Execution Risk: Multiple simultaneous product launches (affordable model, Cybercab, Optimus) increase operational complexity.

GUIDANCE

Tesla does not provide specific revenue guidance. Management guided for "slight growth" in vehicle deliveries in 2024 versus 2023 (1.81 million). For 2025, Tesla expects 20-30% growth in vehicle deliveries, contingent on affordable model ramp.
